BlairSnake

My browser based clone of the classic Nokia game, Snake, with an 8-bit spin. Ironically, cannot be played on mobile due to modern smartphones' lack of arrow keys. Please open on a laptop or desktop for the best experience!

Built with HTML/CSS, HTML Canvas animation and JS/jQuery. I created the 8-bit snake gif and 8-bit food images with Photoshop.

Jeopardy with Friends - Live!

A chat based Jeopardy game using socket.io. This was a group project, built in 1 week. I was responsible for managing version control on Git, as well as the design. I also built a large part of the game/chat logic.

Built with Node.js, HTML/CSS, jQuery, Socket.io, Materialize.css, and the jservice.io API, which provided the Jeopardy questions.

IONIC MOBILE • NODE.JS • ANGULAR

FluffMates

An iOS mobile app using the Petfinder API to allow you to search for pets to adopt in your zip code. The idea came from a friend of mine who wanted "Postmates, but for adopting pets." Users can log in and select their three favorite pets, then click a button to have them delivered! (Next step is to hire a fleet of delivery drivers.)

Built with the Ionic mobile framework, Angular, HTML/CSS, Node.js and MongoDB.

I studied Art History and Business at UCSD, and eventually became a graphic designer and social media professional in the fashion industry in DTLA. I was always interested in coding, ever since I started tinkering around with HTML and CSS back in the Myspace days. I took some Computer Science classes in college, and early last year I started teaching myself how to build websites. I focused mostly on HTML, CSS, JS/jQuery and responsive design techniques , with a little bit of PHP as well.

Eventually, I felt that I would benefit from a more structured learning environment, and I was ready to take the leap into developing! I settled on General Assembly’s 12 week long Web Development Immersive bootcamp, which I completed in December 2016. We learned the full MEAN stack, and Ruby on Rails. And for my final project I taught myself the Ionic mobile framework, and built a mobile app using the PetFinder API.

Coding combines my love for good design, building things, being creative, solving problems, and drinking way too much coffee in ways I never imagined! I can’t wait to find my home on a dev team near you. I’m also available for freelance or contract work remotely, or in the LA area.
